Ukraine Invasion: A Sad Reminder

The invasion of Ukraine is an unwelcome reminder that my own father, Albert, was subjected to similar ordeals imposed by a Russian dictator. His family were targeted by Stalin's Repressions.

The turmoil started with his grandparents being dispossessed. For a child that was difficult to comprehend. However, nothing could prepare him for the programme of genocide openly planned by Soviet leaders.

My Father's Story

c.1930 Niiskovitsa, Ingria, Russia

In blue stripes my father Albert Georgijev poses next to his mother Aliide. She has good reason to look anxious: Stalin's propaganda machine has begun to demonise an entire demographic encouraging a hatred of so-called " enemies of the people " which may include this family.

Under Stalin's rule children were not spared.

L to R: Ludmilla, Rosalie, Salme, Aliide, Niina and Albert.

Madis and Maarja Noole (Aliide's parents, front left) were successful farmers in Markuse: they provided regular employment and sold produce in Gatchina and St Petersburg. For these 'crimes' they were targeted by Stalin's NKVD. Identified as 'Kulaks' their farm was confiscated and both sentenced to a life in exile. However, there is a surprising addendum to this.

Aliide was brought up in a large farm house which was near Markuse village. She was a skilled seamstress. She had trained in Narva, stayed with Estonian relatives and left with a positive impression, vowing to return. Being a proud owner of a sewing machine Aliide was able to provide a service of tailoring and repairing cloth. Albert could recall how they as children were taught to sew.

Vassili Georgijev was an orphan raised by a widow in Niiskovitsa, Ingria. Details of his origin are scant and the Bulgarian surname a mystery. In the wedding photograph Aliide is surrounded by Aliide's family.

Madis and Maarja were not exiled to the East. When the police came to collect them they could not be located. Instead, the authorities isolated them nearby Volosova. Albert's sister Salme, recalls that two cats, Ruti and Ints, and a dog, Pontu, were abandoned at the Markuse farmhouse. Despite warnings Madis and Maarja made clandestine visits to their children. Image of eviction. Source: Yury Dmitriev.

After years of building up his farm in Niiskovitsa Vassili lost everything to the Collectives. He was allowed a solitary cow, put to work as a labourer on his own land and paid in grain which had to be taken to the mill to be ground. The humiliation was hard to bear.
